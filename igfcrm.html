Problem

Customers tracked orders, but could not understand influencer relationships: who was gifted, how often, how much was spent, and which influencers posted.

Goal

Build a relationship-centric CRM view that rolls up gifting history and enables post tracking through a simple, scalable workflow.

Constraints

  • Influencers cannot be duplicated
  • Influencers may exist across multiple stores
  • Post scraping adds platform complexity
  • Phase-based delivery needed

Overview

A quick read that sets up the why, what, and what you shipped.

The key reframing

Orders are transactions. Influencers are relationships. The product needed a contact-style model so gifting and posts could roll up cleanly into one “relationship view.”

Process and Decision Rationale

Keep this section tight. Each decision should connect to a user question and a system constraint.

Why a table-first CRM

  • Scales better than card grids for large influencer lists
  • Supports fast scanning and comparison (CRM muscle memory)
  • Creates a stable backbone for filters, tags, segments later

Why Phase 1 excludes posts

  • Posts add scraping complexity and platform-specific edge cases
  • Phase 1 uses high-confidence data from orders and influencer identity
  • Phase split reduces risk while still delivering customer value fast

Reflection

Keep this practical. Hiring managers want to see judgment and tradeoffs.

What I learned

  • Most “feature requests” are really data-model problems in disguise.
  • Progressive disclosure keeps power features from overwhelming the default workflow.
  • Trust in CRM data depends on preventing duplication more than adding fields.

What I would do next

  • Add tagging and notes to capture relationship context.
  • Introduce segmentation (top spend, high post rate, high engagement).
  • Design basic filters and sorting as the list grows.
  • Define fallback behavior when scraping fails.

Outcome

Delivered an interactive CRM concept in Figma to support customer demos and provide a realistic phased direction for implementation.
